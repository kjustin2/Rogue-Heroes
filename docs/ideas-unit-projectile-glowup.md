# Unit, Projectile & VFX Glow-Up — Ideas

Rogue-Heroes reads at a strategic zoom where color is team-locked, so identity has to live in silhouette, proportion, motion, and where the glow strips sit — not in tint. These ideas push every unit toward a one-shape-per-role read (black-silhouette test), give each weapon class a projectile you can ID from its streak alone, and keep FX landing on the target instead of the screen. Meshy stays strictly inside this repo's sanctioned hard-surface hull/prop scope with a procedural fallback on every item — infantry, characters, and projectiles are never Meshy.

**One grounding fact these ideas lean on:** `paintPart` normalizes a mesh's color to its team unless the mesh is tagged `{ accent: true }` in the `this.box/cylinder/sphere` opts. So any per-kind color/crest that must survive the strategic-zoom team read has to be an `accent` mesh (as the existing kits already are); pure structural greebles inherit team hue and read only by shape. Reproportioning is always safe — it's geometry, not color.

## Unit Distinctiveness

1. **Give each infantry role ONE dominant silhouette primitive in `buildSoldier` (worldRenderer.ts:1125), reproportioning the shared chassis before attaching kit.** Heavy = widen the torso barrel + planted wide leg stance (barrel/trapezoid); scout = narrow torso + forward-leaning crouch (thin wedge); sniper = tall + kneeling stance with the rifle extending the horizontal line; medic/engineer = rounded backpack hump. Today all 12 infantry kinds share the one chassis assembled at 1131–1146 and only differ by kit attached in the `if (entity.kind === ...)` ladder, so they clone at 32px. Reproportion per-kind by scaling the shared `this.box`/`this.cylinder` torso+leg sizes inside that ladder, not just adding props — a reproportion is a variant, a recolor is not.

2. **Put the identity in the top 25% of the silhouette (headgear/crest channel).** At tactics cam the helmet outline is the most reliably visible region. Each kind already supplies its own helmet on top of the shared `head` sphere (1145) — extend that with a distinct head/shoulder crest tagged to the `head` part: sniper ghillie hood spikes, heavy dome + ear cans, droneop antenna rig, medic cross-crest, mortar spotter visor. Where the crest needs a non-team color, tag it `{ accent: true }` (as the existing sniper ghillie at 1158–1159 does) or it will just take team hue. These clear cover and stay readable when the body is occluded.

3. **One asymmetric "reading anchor" per unit.** Symmetric greebles still read as a clone row; a single off-center feature breaks it. Add one asymmetric mesh per kind on an existing part id: engineer's slung tool arm (`pack`), striker's single shoulder pauldron over the Arc Blade arm (`rifle` side — the striker already has one buckler arm at 1175, lean into the asymmetry), grenadier's bandolier across one shoulder, sapper's one-side demo satchel. Attach on the relevant part so damage tinting/hiding still tracks it.

4. **Enforce a scale hierarchy across the roster so the army reads at a glance.** Snap unit height/footprint into distinct tiers — recon (scout/sniper) shortest and thinnest, line infantry mid, heavy/flamer/sapper bulkier, vehicles their own tier. Drive it from a small `infantryProportions(kind)` table sited next to `infantryPalette` (worldRenderer.ts:2985), read by the `buildSoldier` chassis sizing. Elites/veterans from the campaign roster size up visibly within their tier.

5. **Make the drone operator's actual drone the silhouette, not the operator.** The droneop's identity is the hovering recon drone it fields — give the drone a distinct cross/quad-rotor top-down outline (readable at strategic zoom) with a slow rotor-bob idle, built as its own procedural group in the droneop branch of `buildSoldier`. The operator stays a normal trooper with an antenna crest (per #2); the eye goes to the drone. This stays procedural — the drone moves and the sim anchors to it, so it is out of Meshy scope.

6. **Differentiate the three vehicles by hull proportion + turret placement, not just kit.** Tank = low wide hull, big central turret; APC = tall boxy troop-carrier hull, small offset cupola; artillery = long chassis, rear-mounted high-angle barrel. The sim part bag comes from `createVehicle` (damageModel.ts:162); the procedural *mesh* is `buildTank` (worldRenderer.ts:908, shared by all three). Set the distinguishing proportions in `buildTank` per `entity.kind` so the three read distinctly even with the asset dir empty — this fallback must hold before the Meshy hulls (#20) layer on top.

7. **Living idles + weight-graded attack timing to kill the amateur "sliding/hovering feet" tell.** The walk cycle already reads `moving`/bob at worldRenderer.ts:839 — add a breathing/weapon-sway idle and per-kind attack timing keyed off weight: heavies get slow anticipation + big overshoot + heavy settle, scouts snappy low-anticipation quick-recovery. Judge with a filmstrip (`npm run vision`), not stills. Foot speed must match ground speed.

8. **Mandatory hit reactions per part (flinch/stagger), respecting hit direction.** When `applyDamage` damages or destroys a part, the renderer should play a directional flinch on that unit (lean away from the shot vector) and a stagger on mobility-part loss. A unit that eats a hit without reacting breaks cause-and-effect. Drive it off the damage/`VisualEvent` stream consumed in `syncEffects` (worldRenderer.ts:2246) so it layers over locomotion (partial-body), never T-poses the unit in place.

## Projectiles & Impact VFX

9. **Shape each `ProjectileKind` as a distinct side-silhouette in `makeProjectileModel` (worldRenderer.ts:2663).** Rifle = short bright dash/streak; shell = fat teardrop body with a trailing ribbon; bolt = elongated capsule with a leading spike; grenade = stubby body + smoke ribbon. The meshes already exist per branch (`shell-body`/`bolt-core`/`rifle-slug`/`grenade-body`) — tune their pooled `projectileGeometry(key)` proportions (worldRenderer.ts:3373) so you can ID the weapon from the projectile alone. Thin cores, not fat goo.

10. **Three concentric emissive bands on every projectile core (near-white hot core → saturated mid glow → faint additive halo).** Keep the core THIN and push it over-white-hot so the bloom stack does the work — the bolt branch (2715–2742) already layers core/shell/ring and is the template. Extend the same layering across the rifle/shell/grenade branches via `projectileMaterial(key,color,opacity,additive)` (worldRenderer.ts:3417) + the mid color from `projectileStyle` (2615). Hue stays team-locked; the band structure + shape carry per-weapon identity.

11. **Trail length/thickness encodes speed and weight, via a tapered ribbon not a uniform tube.** Rifle/bolt = long thin fast-fading streak; shell/grenade = short thick trail. Trails today pool `projectileGeometry("ember")` puffs (e.g. 2188) — swap to a tapered trail geometry (leading-edge bright → tail transparent), sized per `sourceKind` in the projectile branch. Scroll a noise mask along the trail UV so it shimmers ("boil") instead of sitting static.

12. **Add a real `"flame"` ProjectileKind so the flamer stops firing an invisible rifle round.** Today the flamer fires an ordinary projectile and reads only via `projectileStyle` color plus the `burnZones` seeded in `removeProjectile` (sim.ts:2306). Add `"flame"` to the `ProjectileKind` union (sim.ts:230), route the flamer to it in `projectileKind` (sim.ts:3940), add a `sourceKind === "flamer"` branch in `makeProjectileModel` building a ragged short-range flame cone (a few overlapping cones with scrolling noise), and add its pooled keys to `projectileGeometry`. Also touch `projectileArcHeight`/`projectileProximityRadius`/`explosiveBlast` (sim.ts:3997/4010/4065) so the new kind resolves sanely. Keep it short and grounded — no screen-fill.

13. **The muzzle → tracer → impact three-beat combo, with impact as the loudest beat AT the target.** `makeMuzzleFlash` (worldRenderer.ts:2824) stays small/additive at the barrel (scale to weapon power, 1–2 frames + smoke puff). The tracer is the projectile (#9–11). The impact (in `syncEffects`, worldRenderer.ts:2246) is the loudest: concentric spark ring + directional debris spray aligned to the surface normal + a brief scorch — tuned louder than the muzzle so the *result* reads. Never a full-screen flash.

14. **Surface-aware impacts for material cause-and-effect.** Different hit surface = different impact: metal (vehicle hull/turret, `wall`) = sparks; infantry/dirt = puff + debris; structure = concrete chips. Branch the impact FX on the hit entity's `EntityKind`/`PartRole` (both available via `isVehicleKind`/`isInfantryKind`/`isDefenseKind`, already imported at worldRenderer.ts:3) so the impact tells you what got hit. Crit/kill impacts escalate the ring/spark count but stay contained.

15. **Hit-confirm on ≥3 channels for every landed shot.** Pair the impact VFX (#13) with a target micro-flinch (#8) and a camera micro-kick/hitstop, so a hit never reads as a miss. Route the impact `VisualEvent` so it triggers the flinch on the hit unit and a small trauma kick — audit that no path spawns particles only.

16. **Ballistic arc telegraph as a terrain-conforming decal ring + animated dashed parabola + landing ghost, for mortar/artillery/grenadier.** Previz the indirect shot before resolve: a dashed parabola with dashes flowing toward the target, an AoE ring hugging the heightfield (projected via `terrainHeightAt`, sim.ts, never a floating flat disc), and an impact-splash ghost. Add a new `VisualEvent["type"]` (e.g. `"arc-telegraph"`, the union at sim.ts:191) emitted via the private `effect(...)` helper (sim.ts:3693) and handle it as a case in `syncEffects`. Encode danger vs. safe by shape/hatching/pulse, not just red/green (colorblind + `highContrastTeams`).

17. **Audit that the drawn arc matches the projectile's REAL path over cover.** Owner's collision/plausibility bar: the telegraph reticle must clear the same hills the shell clears, and the shell must actually arc over cover it's shown clearing. The arc telegraph (#16) should sample the same trajectory the sim resolves through `firstGroundBetweenShot` (sim.ts:3844) / `firstCoverBetweenShot` (sim.ts:2860) with the same `arcHeight` from `projectileArcHeight` — re-audit whenever movement or terrain math changes.

18. **Warm the new impact/flame/scorch/telegraph materials in `warmUp()` after staging new material kinds.** Adding `"flame"` cones (#12), tapered trails (#11), and the arc telegraph (#16) introduces new material kinds — register them in `stage.ts` `warmUp()` so the first flamer shot or first mortar arc doesn't stall the composer on a shader relink at the menu↔battle flip.

19. **Keep the scorch decal FIFO-cap honest as new blast sources land.** The scorch decal (`_scorchMaterial()`/`_scorchGeometry()`, worldRenderer.ts:3187/3228) is FIFO-capped at 40 via `scorchedIds` and spawned in `syncEffects` (2349). Flame burn-zones and cluster bomblets will spam blasts — confirm they route through the same capped path (or get their own smaller cap) so a long Domination match doesn't accrete unbounded ground decals or leak `userData.shared` geometry.

## Meshy Pipeline Uses

Sanctioned scope only: hard-surface vehicle/structure/prop hulls + cosmetic retexture skins. The full `ModelKey` set is exactly `tank | apc | artillery | hq | turret | barricade | sandbags | crates | rock` (models.ts:19) — nothing outside it is eligible. Every item keeps its procedural fallback: `instantiate(key)` returns `null` on a missing/failed load and `buildFromModel` drops through to the procedural builder, so dev/CI/empty-asset-dir all still run.

20. **Upgrade the three vehicle hulls (tank/apc/artillery) with distinct-proportion Meshy GLBs (~30 credits each, ~90 total).** All three are in `ModelKey` with `TARGET_SIZE` slots (3.4/3.2/4.2, models.ts:25). Generate hulls that hold the silhouette hierarchy from #6 (low-wide tank, tall-boxy APC, long rear-barrel artillery). `buildFromModel` falls back to `buildTank` on `null`; `normalize()` recenters/rescales to `TARGET_SIZE[key]`; `PICK_PROXY_LAYOUTS` (worldRenderer.ts:3111) supplies the invisible raycast part boxes over the single-mesh GLB. Fallback proportions (in `buildTank`) must be set first so the read survives an empty asset dir.

21. **Refine the structure hulls — `hq` and `turret` — for a stronger base read (~30 credits each, ~60 total).** Both are sanctioned `ModelKey`s. A chunkier HQ and a readable gun-turret silhouette anchor the battlefield. `exturret` (Mortar Turret) deliberately stays procedural — `modelKeyFor` (worldRenderer.ts:3085) returns `null` for it after two Meshy attempts produced sprawled/flat tubes (see the comment at 3092). Leave it; do not add it to `ModelKey`.

22. **Set-dressing prop hulls: `rock`, `crates`, `sandbags`, `barricade` (~30 credits each; do the cheapest coverage first).** These are static cover/terrain dressing — the safe center of the scope. Generate 1–2 shape variants per prop for silhouette variety along cover lines. Each keeps its `COVER_PROFILES`-driven procedural fallback (damageModel.ts:613, consumed by maps.ts) so cover still spawns with no GLB present.

23. **Cosmetic skin packs as Meshy RETEXTURES, not new meshes (~10 credits each).** Extend the existing winter pack pattern via `scripts/retexture-models.mjs` (reuses the `.meshy.json` sidecar task ids) to add e.g. a "desert" or "urban" hull skin: `<key>-<skin>.glb` (e.g. `tank-desert.glb`), swapped by `setModelSkin(skin)` (models.ts:46). A missing skinned file silently falls back to the standard hull (the `ensureLoad` failed-skin path bumps `version` so `instantiate` re-resolves, models.ts:117). Retextures never touch team-color logic — team hue is still `paintPart`'s masked zone; the skin only reskins the neutral structural zone.

24. **`--balance` preflight before any batch, and cache raw GLBs in `assets-raw/`.** Run `node --env-file=.env scripts/build-models.mjs --balance` to confirm credit headroom before generating: a full vehicle+structure+prop refresh (#20–22) is 9 hulls ≈ 270 credits against the monthly cap. Generation is offline only; `MESHY_API_KEY` stays in gitignored `game/.env`.

25. **What Meshy must NOT do here (hard boundary).** No infantry/characters, no weapons-in-hand, no drones, no projectiles/tracers/flame cones/impact FX, no VFX geometry, and no AI text-to-image for any game art. All 12 infantry kinds, the droneop's drone, `exturret`, `wall`, and every projectile/`VisualEvent` stay procedural in `worldRenderer.ts`. Anything the sim anchors to that moves = procedural, always.

**Cost posture:** Items 1–19 are procedural code changes with zero asset/credit cost and no new hard dependency. The Meshy items (20–24) run ~10 credits per cosmetic retexture and ~30 per hull (a full 9-hull refresh ≈ 270 credits) — gate each batch behind a `--balance` preflight against the monthly cap, and every GLB ships with a procedural fallback so nothing here is a hard dependency.

**Balance-risk note:** the identity items (1–8) and projectile items (9–14) are read/clarity changes and should be balance-neutral by construction — but #4 (scale hierarchy) and #6 (vehicle reproportion) change silhouette *and* the pick-proxy hit footprints (`PICK_PROXY_LAYOUTS`), so re-verify selection/raycast and cover-occlusion after resizing. #12 (real `flame` kind) is the one genuine balance surface: routing the flamer through new `projectileArcHeight`/`projectileProximityRadius`/`explosiveBlast` values can shift its effective range/AoE — hold those numbers to the current flamer behavior unless a retune is intended, and add a sim unit test pinning flame range/burn-zone seeding so RNG call order and damage stay deterministic.
